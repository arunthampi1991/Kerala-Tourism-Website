Pathanamthitta

(Est. - 1 Nov 1982)

In 1982 the district of Pathanamthitta was formed and the town of Pathanamthitta was named its headquarters. It is surrounded by the districts of Kottayam, Idukki, Kollam and Alappuzha on all directions. It is an inland district. It is predominantly an agricultural district growing rubber and tea the most. The shrine at Sabarimala is a star attraction and about 10 million pilgrims visit it every year.

These are two unique destinations to visit in Pathanamthitta.

Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls

Located on the western ghats of the Sahyadri Range famous for the waterfalls, Perunthenaruvi falls are an attractive tourist destination in South India. These beautiful falls are located in Vechoochira panchayat, Pathanamthitta district of Kerala state and the stream joins the Pamba river.

Gavi

Gavi is a beautiful and pristine location in Pathanamthitta that is an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. The Eco-tourism initiative in Gavi has transformed it into a nature lovers paradise, with opportunities to explore the Gavi lake, the adjoining forests, cascading waterfalls, cardamom plantations and the rich flora and fauna, with the help of the local populace.
